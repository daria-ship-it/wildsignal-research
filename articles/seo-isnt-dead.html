No, SEO Isn’t Dead

By Andy PrayOctober 21, 2025
Originally published at Wild Signal — wildsignal.agency ↗

TL;DR

SEO isn't dead, but it's evolving. Traditional search still matters, but AI-powered answers are eating into search traffic. Smart brands are doing both: optimizing for Google AND optimizing for AI citation. The brands treating GEO and SEO as competing priorities are making a strategic mistake.

What's actually happening to SEO?

Traditional search volume is declining for informational queries as AI answers capture them directly. Transactional and navigational queries still flow through traditional search. SEO remains valuable—but its domain is shrinking while AI search's domain is growing.

How do SEO and GEO complement each other?

SEO builds the web presence and domain authority that GEO builds upon. High-authority domains earn more AI citations. SEO-optimized content is often well-structured enough to be citable. Many GEO best practices—clear structure, authoritative claims, good source reputation—overlap with SEO best practices.

What does a combined SEO + GEO strategy look like?

Content created for SEO should be evaluated for citability. Technical SEO investments (site authority, backlink profiles) transfer to GEO as authority signals. Measurement must expand to include AI citation metrics alongside traditional SEO metrics.

The Bottom Line

SEO and GEO are complementary, not competing. Brands that treat them as an integrated discovery strategy will outperform those that abandon SEO for GEO or ignore GEO to protect existing SEO investments.
