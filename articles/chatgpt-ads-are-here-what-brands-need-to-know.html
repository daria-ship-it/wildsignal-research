ChatGPT Has Ads Now. Here’s What That Actually Changes.

By Andy PrayFebruary 10, 2026
Originally published at Wild Signal — wildsignal.agency ↗

OpenAI launched advertising in ChatGPT on February 9, 2026. After years of positioning ads as a "last resort," the company reversed course—introducing sponsored links inside AI-generated answers. This changes the strategic calculus for brands operating in AI search.

What exactly launched?

ChatGPT now surfaces sponsored links within responses. These aren't traditional display ads—they appear as recommended sources or actions inside AI-generated answers, blending with organic citations. The format is closer to native content than banner advertising.

Does this replace the need for organic GEO?

No. Sponsored placement in AI answers is an addition to the ecosystem, not a replacement for organic authority. AI systems still generate organic citations based on content quality, source trust, and relevance. Paid placement may increase visibility for specific queries, but it doesn’t build the underlying authority that drives organic citation.

What does this mean for brands with strong GEO positioning?

Brands with established organic citation authority are in a stronger position. Paid placement amplifies visibility, but authority determines how AI describes and contextualizes a brand. Brands cited organically for expertise will be described differently than brands appearing only through paid placement.

Will all AI platforms follow?

Perplexity introduced advertising in 2024. Google AI Overviews incorporates Shopping ads. The ad model is becoming standard across AI search. Brands need both a paid AI search strategy and an organic GEO strategy—just as they did in traditional search.

The Bottom Line

ChatGPT ads confirm that AI search is maturing into a dual-track environment: paid visibility and organic authority. Brands that have invested in GEO now have a foundation that paid placement can amplify rather than replace.
