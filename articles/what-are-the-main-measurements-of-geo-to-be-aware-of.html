What Are the Main Measurements of GEO to Be Aware Of?

By Daria DuboisOctober 21, 2025
Originally published at Wild Signal — wildsignal.agency ↗

TL;DR

The key GEO metrics are citation rate (how often your content gets cited), citation share (your citations vs. competitors), brand sentiment in AI (how AI describes you), coverage breadth (how many queries you answer), and average position (where you appear in AI answers). These replace traditional SEO metrics like rankings and click-through rates.

Why do traditional metrics fail for GEO?

Traditional SEO metrics measure clicks, rankings, and impressions on search result pages. AI search often provides answers without generating clicks. New measurement frameworks are required that track how AI uses your content rather than how users click on it.

What is citation rate?

Citation rate measures how frequently AI systems reference your content as a source across a defined query set. Higher citation rates indicate stronger source authority. This is the foundational GEO metric.

What is citation share?

Citation share measures your citations relative to competitors across the same query set. A brand with 40% citation share in its category owns 40% of AI-mediated category discovery. Share is the competitive metric that matters most.

How is average position measured?

Average position tracks where your brand appears within AI answers—first, second, or later. First position implies top recommendation; later positions imply secondary consideration. Tracking position changes reveals whether your authority is improving or declining.

The Bottom Line

GEO measurement is an emerging discipline. Brands that establish measurement baselines now will be positioned to demonstrate ROI and track progress as AI search matures.
