Why One GEO Strategy Does Not Fit All Models

By Daria DuboisJanuary 6, 2026
Originally published at Wild Signal — wildsignal.agency ↗

A common mistake brands make in AI search is assuming one GEO strategy works across all large language models. Different AI models retrieve information differently, trust different sources, and reinforce authority in distinct ways. Treating AI search as a single surface leads to inconsistent visibility across platforms.

Why do different AI models behave differently?

Each major AI model—ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude—uses different training data, different retrieval architectures, and different trust signals. A source that ChatGPT cites heavily may be underweighted by Gemini. Platform-specific citation behavior requires platform-aware strategy.

What are the key differences between platforms?

Differences include retrieval frequency (how often each platform searches vs. uses training data), source trust hierarchies (which publications each platform weights most), citation style (how platforms surface sources), and temporal recency weighting (how much each platform favors recent content).

How should brands adapt strategy across models?

Start with an audit: run consistent query sets across platforms and record which sources are cited for your category. Identify gaps—sources present in some platforms but not others. Build authority in underrepresented sources for platforms where you are less visible.

Should brands prioritize one platform?

Prioritize the platform that most influences your customers' decisions, but don't ignore others. Market share shifts. Platform-only strategies create concentration risk. Build cross-platform authority as a strategic foundation, with tactical emphasis where customer impact is highest.

The Bottom Line

AI search is a multi-platform environment. Brands that audit and adapt across models will build more resilient and comprehensive AI visibility than those optimizing for a single platform.
