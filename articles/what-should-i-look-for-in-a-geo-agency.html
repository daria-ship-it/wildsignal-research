What Should I Look For in a GEO Agency?

Andy Pray December 19, 2025 6 min read

The GEO space is moving fast, and the agency landscape is still taking shape. Some firms are genuine specialists. Many more are traditional SEO agencies that have added "AI search optimization" to their service list without changing much underneath.

If you're evaluating agencies to help your brand show up in AI-generated responses, here's what to look for—and what to watch out for.

1. They Can Explain Their Methodology in Plain Language

Ask any GEO agency: "How do you help brands get cited in AI responses?" A strong agency will walk you through their approach—content structure, authority signals, platform-specific tactics, and how they measure progress.

A weak answer looks like: "We optimize your content for AI" without specifics. Or: "We use proprietary techniques" without being able to describe what that means.

GEO is a young field, so some ambiguity is normal. But the best agencies have tested hypotheses, documented results, and can articulate what they've learned.

2. They Track AI Visibility Specifically—Not Just Organic Traffic

Traditional SEO agencies track rankings, clicks, and impressions. A GEO agency needs to track something different: whether and how often your brand appears in AI-generated answers.

Ask them what tools they use. Options include Profound, Otterly, AIM Monitor, or custom prompt-based testing frameworks. If they're only measuring organic traffic and calling it "AI visibility," that's a red flag—AI citations don't always generate clicks.

3. They've Worked With Your Type of Content

GEO results vary significantly depending on industry, content type, and target queries. An agency with experience in B2B software behaves differently from one focused on consumer brands or professional services.

Ask for case studies or examples from clients in your category. Specifically ask: what types of queries did you help them rank for, and in which AI platforms did their visibility improve?

4. They Understand the Difference Between Platforms

Perplexity, ChatGPT, Claude, and Google AI Overviews all work differently. An agency that treats them as a monolith probably hasn't done the platform-specific work to understand which content structures and authority signals matter on each.

Look for agencies that can speak to the differences—and who have a view on where your content is most likely to get traction based on your category and audience.

5. They're Honest About What Can't Be Controlled

No agency can guarantee AI citations. The models make their own decisions, and those decisions aren't fully transparent. Any agency promising guaranteed visibility in ChatGPT or Perplexity within a specific timeframe is overselling.

What a legitimate agency can promise: a content and authority-building strategy based on current best evidence, measured against real AI visibility metrics, with iterative refinement based on results.

What to Watch Out For

  • AI SEO rebrands without substance: Agencies that changed their name or homepage but didn't change their methodology.
  • Citation guarantees: Anyone promising a specific number of citations in a specific timeframe is guessing.
  • Training-data manipulation claims: Some agencies claim they can get your content into model training data. This is largely unverifiable and often misleading.
  • No measurement plan: If the agency can't describe how they'll measure AI visibility before the engagement starts, that's a problem.

What good looks like: A GEO agency should come to an initial conversation with a point of view on your current AI visibility—where you're showing up, where you're not, and why. If they haven't done any pre-call research on your brand, that tells you something.

The Right Questions to Ask

  1. How do you currently track AI citations for your clients?
  2. What's your content framework for improving AI visibility?
  3. Which AI platforms do you focus on, and why?
  4. Can you show me a before/after for a client in my industry?
  5. How long does it typically take to see measurable improvements?
  6. What's your process when a strategy isn't working?

The answers won't be perfect—GEO is new enough that no agency has a fully mature playbook. But you're listening for intellectual honesty, a measurement-first mindset, and real evidence of testing and iteration.
