How PR Must Evolve for AI Discovery

By Daria DuboisJanuary 6, 2026
Originally published at Wild Signal — wildsignal.agency ↗

Public relations was built for human discovery—journalists, readers, and human gatekeepers who made editorial decisions about coverage. AI discovery operates differently: AI systems evaluate source authority, content structure, and claim consistency rather than narrative appeal.

How does AI evaluate PR coverage differently than humans?

AI systems assess coverage for authority signals, specificity, and consistency. A vague profile piece in a major publication may carry less citation weight than a detailed feature in a niche authoritative outlet. AI evaluates what is said, not just where it appears.

What does PR strategy need to change?

PR strategy must shift from awareness-driven to authority-driven. Pitches should aim for substantive coverage that defines category positioning, uses specific attributable language, and appears in sources AI trusts. Volume of placements matters less than depth and authority of coverage.

How should brands brief journalists for GEO value?

Briefing journalists to use specific, consistent language about your brand creates durable citation signals. Providing clear, quotable definitions of your category positioning gives journalists precise language to use—language that AI will then cite consistently across multiple sources.

What outcomes should PR measure for GEO?

Beyond traditional metrics (impressions, reach), PR should measure citation authority: whether coverage appears in sources AI cites, whether coverage uses positioning language consistently, and whether AI descriptions of the brand shift following earned media campaigns.

The Bottom Line

PR has become a GEO discipline. The most valuable coverage is not the most read—it is the most cited by AI systems building the answers that reach your next customer.
